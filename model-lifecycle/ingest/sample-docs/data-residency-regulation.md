# Data Residency Regulation

## 1. Definitions

**Personal data** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. An identifiable person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, by reference to an identifier such as a name, identification number, location data, online identifier, or one or more factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity of that person.

**Processing** means any operation performed on personal data, whether automated or manual, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination, alignment, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

**Approved processing location** means a data center, computing facility, or infrastructure environment that is physically located within this jurisdiction and operated by an entity that is registered and licensed under the laws of this jurisdiction.

## 2. General Requirement

All personal data of residents of this jurisdiction must be stored and processed at approved processing locations. This requirement applies regardless of the data controller's place of incorporation or principal place of business.

## 3. Transfer Mechanisms

Personal data may be transferred to a processing location outside this jurisdiction only if one of the following conditions is met:

(a) The receiving jurisdiction has been designated as providing an adequate level of data protection by the national data protection authority. The adequacy assessment must consider the rule of law, respect for human rights, relevant legislation, the existence of an independent supervisory authority, and international commitments.

(b) The data controller has put in place appropriate safeguards, including legally binding contractual clauses, binding corporate rules, or an approved certification mechanism, and the data subjects have enforceable rights and effective legal remedies.

(c) The data subject has explicitly consented to the transfer after being informed of the risks, and the transfer is occasional and necessary for the performance of a contract between the data subject and the controller.

## 4. Certification Requirements

Data processing facilities operating within this jurisdiction must obtain certification from the national data protection authority. Certification requires demonstration of: physical security controls, network isolation capabilities, access control mechanisms, audit trail generation, incident response procedures, and staff training programs.

Certification must be renewed every two years. The national data protection authority may conduct unannounced inspections at any time during the certification period.

## 5. AI-Specific Provisions

AI model training that involves personal data must occur at approved processing locations. The resulting model weights are considered a derivative of the personal data used in training and are subject to the same residency requirements unless the data controller can demonstrate, through a formal privacy assessment, that the model weights cannot be used to reconstruct or infer personal data.

AI inference services processing personal data queries must run at approved processing locations. API calls to AI models hosted outside this jurisdiction that include personal data in the prompt, context, or system instructions constitute a cross-border transfer and are subject to the transfer mechanisms in Section 3.

## 6. Penalties

Violations of this regulation may result in administrative fines of up to 5% of the data controller's global annual turnover. Repeated or willful violations may result in suspension of the data controller's processing authorization and referral for criminal prosecution.
