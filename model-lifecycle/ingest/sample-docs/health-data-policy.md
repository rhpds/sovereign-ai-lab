# National Health Data Governance Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes the governance framework for the collection, processing, storage, and sharing of health data within this jurisdiction. It applies to all government agencies, healthcare providers, research institutions, and authorized third parties that handle health-related data.

The objectives of this policy are to protect the fundamental rights of individuals whose health data is processed, to establish clear accountability for data custodians, and to ensure that health data contributes to improved public health outcomes while maintaining citizen trust.

## 2. Data Classification

Health data within this jurisdiction is classified into the following tiers:

**Tier 1 — Public health statistics.** Aggregated, anonymized data that cannot be linked to any individual. This includes disease prevalence rates, vaccination coverage statistics, and healthcare resource utilization summaries. Tier 1 data may be shared freely for public health purposes.

**Tier 2 — De-identified clinical data.** Patient records from which all direct identifiers have been removed, but which retain clinical detail sufficient for research. De-identification must follow the safe harbor method, removing at minimum: names, geographic data below regional level, dates more specific than year, contact information, identification numbers, biometric identifiers, and photographic images.

**Tier 3 — Identifiable personal health data.** Any data that can be linked, directly or indirectly, to a living individual. This includes medical records, genetic data, mental health assessments, substance use records, reproductive health information, and disability status. Tier 3 data receives the highest level of protection under this policy.

## 3. Access Controls

Access to health data shall be governed by the principle of minimum necessary access. No individual or system shall be granted broader access than is required to perform their designated function.

All access to Tier 3 data must be logged in a tamper-evident audit trail. The audit trail must record: the identity of the accessor, the timestamp of access, the scope of data accessed, the stated purpose, and the authorization that permitted the access. Audit trails must be retained for a minimum of seven years.

Automated systems processing Tier 3 data must authenticate using verifiable machine identities. Anonymous or unverifiable system access to Tier 3 data is prohibited.

## 4. Retention Periods

Active medical records shall be retained for the lifetime of the patient plus five years. De-identified research datasets may be retained indefinitely. Public health statistics may be retained indefinitely. Audit trails of data access must be retained for seven years. Temporary processing copies must be destroyed within 30 days of the completion of the processing purpose.

## 5. Cross-Border Transfer Rules

Health data classified as Tier 3 may not be transferred outside this jurisdiction except under the following conditions: (a) the receiving jurisdiction has been assessed as providing an adequate level of data protection, (b) the transfer is necessary for the direct care of the data subject and the data subject has provided explicit consent, or (c) the transfer is required by a valid legal obligation that has been reviewed and approved by the national data protection authority.

All cross-border transfers of health data must be recorded in the national health data register, including the destination jurisdiction, the legal basis for transfer, the scope of data transferred, and the identity of the receiving entity.

## 6. Breach Notification

Any breach involving Tier 3 health data must be reported to the national data protection authority within 72 hours of discovery. Affected individuals must be notified within 14 days if the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights. The notification must include: the nature of the breach, the categories and approximate number of records affected, the likely consequences, and the measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

## 7. Citizen Rights

Every individual has the right to: access their own health data held by any data custodian within this jurisdiction, request correction of inaccurate health data, request erasure of health data that is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected (subject to legal retention requirements), receive a portable copy of their health data in a machine-readable format, and object to automated decision-making based solely on their health data.

Requests under these rights must be fulfilled within 30 calendar days. No fee may be charged for the first request in any 12-month period.

## 8. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in administrative penalties of up to 4% of annual revenue for organizations, personal liability for responsible officers, and suspension or revocation of data processing authorizations. The national data protection authority has the power to conduct audits, issue binding orders, and refer matters for criminal prosecution where appropriate.
